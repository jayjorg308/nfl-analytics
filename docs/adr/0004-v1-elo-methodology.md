# v1 ELO methodology

> **Status: Superseded by ADR-0014.** This ADR's "no margin-of-victory adjustment" decision was revisited and reversed, and the full v1 ELO methodology — including chain initialization, inter-season regression, playoff handling, and tunability posture — has been consolidated into ADR-0014. The body below is preserved as design history.

ELO ratings are computed in-house — there is no canonical community NFL ELO equivalent to nflfastR's EPA (FiveThirtyEight's ratings are discontinued and nflverse does not publish one), so the choice was between computing in-house and skipping ELO entirely. Skipping isn't viable because ELO is central to the team-strength stories v1 surfaces: the season-long trajectory on the Team Page, the "team has high ELO but mediocre record" framing borrowed from a prior MLB project, and the strength-of-context inputs that feed several views.

v1 uses the standard ELO formula with K = 20, home-field advantage of ~50 rating points for win-probability calculation, and **no margin-of-victory adjustment** — MOV-aware ELO is a known refinement that v2 should consider but adds complexity without proportional payoff for v1's analytical claims. Initial seeding regresses each team's prior-season final ELO toward 1500 by a factor of 1/3 (starting point inherited from FiveThirtyEight's published methodology; tunable once a full season of data exists to evaluate it). All ELO values are computed during the weekly ingestion job and snapshotted into `teamWeekStats` alongside other per-week metrics, preserving the temporal-correctness property baked into the rest of the schema.

## Historical bootstrap prerequisite

The "regress prior-season final ELO toward 1500" seeding rule above is load-bearing for meaningful ELO at the start of a new season — and it assumes a prior-season final ELO exists to regress from. A from-scratch ELO bootstrap (all teams starting at 1500 with no history) is not viable for v1 because ELO is iterative and slow to differentiate: with K = 20, the first 8-10 weeks of a fresh start produce ratings that are noise-dominated rather than signal-dominated. That noise period would show up directly on the Slate Dashboard's ELO column and the Team Page's trajectory chart as "everyone is near 1500" — the opposite of what ELO is meant to communicate, and a credibility hit on a user-facing analytical surface.

The prerequisite is therefore a historical backfill computing ELO forward from **N = 5 seasons** before the current one (so for the 2026 season, ELO is computed iteratively over 2021–2025 to establish each team's 2026 Week-0 starting point). Five seasons strikes the balance between giving ELO room to stabilise (≈85 games per team chain) and not letting roster turnover too far in the past dominate the present (NFL rosters refresh substantially over a 5+-year window). The backfill is one-time per historical-range extension; routine weekly ingestion picks up from the backfilled state. See ADR-0008's "Slice 3 sub-phases" for the runtime split between backfill and cron, and why the ordering is strict.
